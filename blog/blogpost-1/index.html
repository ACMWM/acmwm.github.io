This Week in ACM

ACM Interest Meeting

Thursday 1/20/2025 7 PM in ISC 2280 What’s up guys! Welcome to all the new members and a warm hello to past members. Hope you had a great FDOC! If you need some excitement for the coming week, look no further than the first acm meeting of the semester! Show up to find out plans for this semester, meet the new exec and games! It will be a fun time even if you are already a member of the club. (Bring a friend even, everyone is welcome!) General Announcements

&hacks Organizer Application

Organizer applications for &hacks XI are open! We’re looking for committee leads and members to take &hacks to the next level. The organizing team will be composed of the following committees: Sponsorship, Finance, Marketing, Outreach, Technical, and Logistics. If you have any interest in the hackathon planning process, you have a place in the organizing team. It’s your skills and ideas that’ll make a great hackathon!

Join the GroupMe! Have you joined our GroupMe? It’s the fastest way to get updates! (https://groupme.com/join_group/103293306/psGCCdFG)

Data & Tech Career Empowerment Office Hours

Bring your career questions to a series of online Data & Tech Career Empowerment Office Hours hosted by W&M alums Jonny Mills (B.S. ‘17, MSBA ‘18) and Isabel Hirama (B.A. ‘14, MSBA ‘17), starting November 22nd and December 6th: https://www.eventbrite.com/cc/data-tech-career-empowerment-3802983

Jonny and Isabel are a data engineer and data scientist currently living in London, England, who are passionate about the power of innovation for human potential and flourishing. Feel free to read about their backgrounds and connect with them on LinkedIn ahead of the events: https://www.linkedin.com/in/jonny-mills and https://www.linkedin.com/in/isabelhirama

Opportunities

Bytes of Knowledge: A Lunch & Learn Experience with Jefferson Lab Are you curious about exciting career opportunities in the field of computer science? Jefferson Lab invites you to a special Lunch and Learn session designed just for you! You will discover diverse career pathways available, learn about our innovative research, and engage in Q&A with members of our J Lab team to help you succeed in your career. We look forward to seeing you there!

This event will take place in McGlothlin-Street Hall, Room 020.

Scientists worldwide utilize the lab’s unique particle accelerator, known as the Continuous Electron Beam Accelerator Facility (CEBAF), to probe the most basic building blocks of matter - helping us to better understand these particles and the forces that bind them - and ultimately our world.

In addition, the lab capitalizes on its unique technologies and expertise to perform advanced computing and applied research with industry and university partners, and provides programs designed to help educate the next generation in science and technology.

MORE INFO: https://wm-csm.symplicity.com/students/?s=event&ss=is&mode=form&id=61c9376f2b862971d9de70087c395ccd
